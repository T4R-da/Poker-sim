5-CARD DRAW POKER RULES
-----------------------

OBJECTIVE:
The goal is to win the pot by having the best 5-card hand or by 
persuading all other players to fold.

THE SETUP:
1. A standard 52-card deck is used.
2. Players contribute an "Ante" (a small starting bet) into the pot.

THE DEAL:
1. Each player is dealt 5 cards face down, one at a time.
2. Players look at their cards without showing others.

FIRST BETTING ROUND:
1. Starting to the left of the dealer, players may:
   - CHECK: Pass the action to the next player (if no bet is made).
   - BET: Place a wager.
   - CALL: Match a previous player's bet.
   - RAISE: Increase the current bet.
   - FOLD: Discard your hand and leave the round.

THE DRAW:
1. After betting, players may discard up to 3 cards (4 if the 5th 
   card is an Ace).
2. The dealer replaces the discarded cards with new ones from the deck.
3. You may "stand pat" (keep all 5 original cards) if you wish.

SECOND BETTING ROUND:
1. A final round of betting occurs, starting with the first 
   active player to the left of the dealer.

THE SHOWDOWN:
1. If more than one player remains, they reveal their cards.
2. The player with the highest-ranking 5-card hand wins the pot.
3. In case of a tie, the pot is split equally.

TIE-BREAKING:
- If hands are of the same rank (e.g., both have a Pair), the 
  highest pair wins.
- If pairs are identical, the highest "Kicker" (the highest 
  unmatched card) determines the winner.
